Blue Slide Park (2011)
This was Mac Miller's debut studio album, released in 2011. The album features upbeat, catchy tracks that showcase Miller's playful and carefree style. Many of the songs on this album were produced by Miller himself under the pseudonym Larry Fisherman, and the album features guest appearances from artists like Sir Michael Rocks, Casey Veggies, and Lil B. "Blue Slide Park" was a commercial success, debuting at number one on the US Billboard 200 and becoming the first independently-distributed debut album to do so since 1995.
Watching Movies with the Sound Off (2013)
Miller's second studio album, released in 2013. This album marked a departure from the more light-hearted style of "Blue Slide Park", showcasing a darker, more introspective side of Miller. The album features a range of production styles and guest appearances from artists like Earl Sweatshirt, Schoolboy Q, and Action Bronson. "Watching Movies with the Sound Off" received critical acclaim and is often regarded as one of Miller's best works.
Faces (2014)
"Faces" is a mixtape that was released for free in 2014. The mixtape features a range of different styles and moods, with Miller exploring themes of drug use, addiction, and mental health. The mixtape features guest appearances from artists like Earl Sweatshirt, Ab-Soul, and Rick Ross, as well as production from Miller himself and a number of other producers. "Faces" is often regarded as one of Miller's most personal and raw works, and its release helped cement his reputation as a talented and versatile artist.
GO:OD AM (2015)
Miller's third studio album, released in 2015. This album features a mix of upbeat, party tracks and more introspective songs that explore themes of addiction and mental health. The album features guest appearances from artists like Chief Keef, Miguel, and Little Dragon, as well as production from Miller and a number of other producers. "GO:OD AM" was generally well-received by critics and helped solidify Miller's place in the hip-hop scene.

Swimming (2018)
Miller's fifth and final studio album, released just a month before his death in 2018. This album is considered by many to be his best work, with a more mature and reflective tone that explores themes of self-care, growth, and recovery. The album features guest appearances from artists like Thundercat and Snoop Dogg, as well as production from Miller and a number of other producers. "Swimming" was critically acclaimed and received widespread praise for its honesty, vulnerability, and musicality.
Circles (2020)
"Circles" is an album that was released posthumously in January 2020, which was intended to be a companion album to "Swimming". It features introspective and introspective songs that reflect on themes of mental health, self-reflection, and acceptance. "Circles" was completed by producer Jon Brion, who worked with Miller on the album before his passing. The album received critical acclaim and is often seen as a fitting tribute to Miller's legacy.
