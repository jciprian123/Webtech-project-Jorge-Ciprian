Mac Miller, whose birth name was Malcolm James McCormick, was born on January 19, 1992, in the Point Breeze neighborhood of Pittsburgh. He grew up in a multicultural family, with his mother Karen Meyers being Jewish and his father Mark McCormick being Christian. Although Miller and his brother were raised Jewish, Miller attended a Catholic grade school to receive a good education and participate in sports like football and lacrosse. He later attended Winchester Thurston School and graduated from Taylor Allderdice High School.

From a young age, Miller showed a strong interest in music and was self-taught in playing instruments such as piano, guitar, drums, and bass by the age of six. He started rapping at the age of fourteen, after originally wanting to pursue a career as a singer. His love for hip-hop and dedication to his craft quickly grew, with Miller later stating that "Once I hit 15, I got real serious about it and it changed my life completely ... I used to be into sports, play all the sports, go to all the high school parties. But once I found out hip-hop is almost like a job, that's all I did."

Miller released his first mixtape, But My Mackin' Ain't Easy, under the name Easy Mac in 2007 at the age of fifteen. He later changed his name to Mac Miller and released two more mixtapes, The Jukebox: Prelude to Class Clown and The High Life, which garnered him a following and helped establish his career in hip-hop. Miller won two awards at the 2010 Pittsburgh Hip Hop Awards for his music, 21 & Under of the Year and Best Hip Hop Video for "Live Free".

Miller's upbringing in a multicultural family and his exposure to different religions and cultures helped shape his artistic style and worldview. He often incorporated Jewish themes and references into his music and was an advocate for social justice causes. Miller's early musical talents and dedication to his craft propelled him to become a respected and influential artist in the hip-hop community.
